Checkers

Rules

  1. Checkers is played by two players. Each player begins the game with 12 colored discs.
  2. Yellow moves first. Players then alternate moves.
  3. Moves are allowed only on the dark squares, so pieces always move diagonally. Single pieces are always limited to forward moves (toward the opponent).
  4. A piece making a non-capturing move (not involving a jump) may move only one square.
  5. A piece making a capturing move (a jump) leaps over one of the opponent's pieces, landing in a straight diagonal line on the other side. Only one piece may be captured in a single jump; however, that player must continue to capture all pieces until there are no more options.
  6. When a piece is captured, it is removed from the board.
  7. When a piece reaches the furthest row from the player who controls that piece, it is crowned and becomes a king.
  8. Kings are limited to moving diagonally, but may move both forward and backward. (Remember that single pieces, i.e. non-kings, are always limited to forward moves.)
  9. Kings may combine jumps in several directions -- forward and backward -- on the same turn. Single pieces may shift direction diagonally during a multiple capture turn, but must always jump forward (toward the opponent).
  10. A player wins the game when the opponent cannot make a move.
